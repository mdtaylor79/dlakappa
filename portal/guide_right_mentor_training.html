Guide Right vs. Kappa League: Know the Difference

Guide Right is the National Service Initiative of Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc. It encompasses all of the fraternity's youth-oriented programs. Guide Right serves boys and girls from elementary school through college and is focused on the educational and occupational guidance of youth — primarily inspirational and informative in character.

Kappa League — officially the Kappa Leadership Development League — is the flagship initiative of Guide Right. Kappa League is male-only, club-oriented, and focused on middle school and high school students. It is a subset of Guide Right, not its equivalent.

Origins of Guide Right

Guide Right was conceived in 1922 by Brother Leon W. Stewart, the "Father of Guide Right" and Past Western Province Polemarch, and was suggested at the 12th Grand Chapter of Kappa Alpha Psi. Following the St. Louis Alumni Chapter's founding in 1921, Brothers Leon W. Stewart and J. Jerome Peters were assigned to research programs that would assist in developing the youth of the Black community and devise a "meaningful and practical approach" to the problem of Black youth underachievement.

Brother Stewart proposed a program of guidance designated as Guide Right, with the initial purpose of assisting high school seniors in choosing and pursuing useful careers. From its earliest days, Guide Right focused on providing scholarships to needy and talented students and informing young people about professions and career options.

What Guide Right Is: "A program of the educational and occupational guidance of youth, primarily inspirational and informative in character. The purpose of the Guide Right Service Program is to place the training, experience, and interest of successful men at the disposal of youth — developing leadership, creating an achievement mindset, and mentoring."

The Five Objectives of Guide Right

The Guide Right program operates under five core objectives that drive every chapter's youth programming:

1. Create the Next Generation of Leaders via Leadership Development▼

Guide Right's primary mission is to develop leaders — not just followers. Every program activity, from officer elections to community service, is designed to grow each young man's capacity to lead with purpose, integrity, and vision. Leadership development is intentional, structured, and consistent across all phases of programming.

2. Prepare Students for College▼

Guide Right connects youth to college preparation resources beginning in middle school. Activities include college visits, admissions workshops, FAFSA assistance, standardized test prep, and College Signing Day celebrations. The program helps every Kappa Leaguer envision and plan a path to higher education.

3. Mentor Students to College Graduation▼

Guide Right's impact doesn't end at enrollment — it extends through college graduation. Mentoring relationships established in Kappa League continue to support young men through their college years. With 77% of Guide Right students graduating college within 6 years, this long-term investment is transformative.

4. Positively Impact Youth Through Mentoring and Training▼

Beyond college prep, Guide Right shapes the character, confidence, and skills of every young man it touches. Through mentoring, structured training, and positive role modeling, Guide Right gives youth the tools they need to navigate life's challenges — academically, socially, and professionally.

5. Prepare Youth for Academic Success in Middle School, High School, and College▼

Guide Right supports academic excellence at every educational stage. With 98% of Guide Right students graduating high school, the program's cradle-to-career approach creates sustained academic achievement. Study halls, tutoring, and academic accountability are embedded across all programming.

From Guide Right to Kappa League

Kappa League — the Kappa Leadership Development League — was founded on February 12, 1969 by the Los Angeles Alumni Chapter at Alain LeRoy Locke High School. The national body adopted Kappa League in 1970 as the structured, club-based flagship of the Guide Right mission.

Today, each chapter's Guide Right Director oversees the full Guide Right program portfolio while the Kappa League Coordinator manages the day-to-day delivery of Kappa League activities, meetings, and mentoring. Understanding this leadership structure ensures clear accountability at every level.

Mission & Purpose

Kappa League is the youth auxiliary of Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc., operating under the Guide Right mandate. Its mission is to develop the next generation of young leaders by exposing them to positive role models, structured programming, and the values of academic achievement, civic responsibility, and character development.

Kappa League is not a fraternity and Kappa Leaguers are not "Junior Kappas." Membership in Kappa League does not confer any fraternal status or future membership guarantee in Kappa Alpha Psi.

Important Distinction: Kappa Leaguers must never be referred to as "Junior Kappas." This language misrepresents the program and blurs the distinct identity of Kappa League as a youth development program, not a fraternal pipeline.

Phase Deep Dives

Phase 1 — Self-Identity & Purpose▼

This foundational phase helps young men develop a clear sense of who they are. Topics include discipline (managing behavior and time), assurance (building confidence and self-worth), self-awareness (understanding strengths and weaknesses), and appearance (professional dress and grooming). Mentors should create a safe space for honest self-reflection and goal articulation.

Phase 2 — Training▼

Phase 2 equips Kappa Leaguers with the academic and career tools they need to succeed. Academic excellence sessions encourage GPA improvement and study habits. Career choice activities expose members to diverse professions. Preparation covers resume writing, interview skills, and goal-setting. Organization teaches time management and personal productivity.

Phase 3 — Competition▼

This phase teaches Kappa Leaguers how to compete constructively. Politics covers civic engagement and understanding governmental systems. Career advancement addresses navigating workplace dynamics. Sports fosters teamwork, sportsmanship, and the role athletics can play in development and scholarship access.

Phase 4 — Social▼

Phase 4 develops the social competencies every young man needs. Religion explores spiritual identity and values. Arts & Entertainment exposes members to creative expression and cultural appreciation. Communication sharpens verbal and written skills. Etiquette training prepares members for professional and formal social settings.

Phase 5 — Health Education▼

This phase promotes physical and emotional wellness. Physical fitness encourages healthy habits. Sex education provides age-appropriate, factual health information. Drug awareness presents the consequences of substance use. Health & safety covers first aid basics and personal safety awareness. All content must be delivered in an age-appropriate, chapter-approved manner.

Phase 6 — Economic Empowerment▼

Phase 6 builds financial literacy and entrepreneurial thinking. Topics include budgeting, saving and investing, banking basics, and entrepreneurship. Young men explore how to start and manage businesses, understand credit, and build long-term financial goals. Guest speakers from business and finance are highly encouraged.

Phase 7 — College & Career▼

The culminating phase prepares Kappa Leaguers for life after high school. Activities include college visits, admissions workshops, FAFSA completion support, scholarship research, and career exploration panels. Mentors play a critical role in connecting members with higher education resources and helping them visualize their futures.

Mentor Eligibility Requirements

Not every brother automatically qualifies to serve as a Kappa Men(tor). The following requirements must be satisfied before any Kappa Man works directly with Kappa Leaguers:

  • ✓Age: Must be at least 21 years of age
  • ✓Background Check: Must successfully complete a Tier 3 background check (no disqualifying criminal history)
  • ✓Clean Criminal Record: No felony convictions; no crimes against minors; no sexual offenses
  • ✓Youth Protection Training: Must complete Boy Scouts of America Youth Protection Training (or equivalent approved training)
  • ✓1-Year Commitment: Must commit to serve for a minimum of one full program year
  • ✓Valid Driver's License: Required for mentors who may transport Kappa Leaguers
  • ✓Good Standing: Must be a financial member in good standing with Kappa Alpha Psi
  • ✓Acknowledgment Form: Must sign the Do's and Don'ts Acknowledgment as required for Guide Right Certification
No Exceptions: All eligibility requirements are mandatory. No Kappa Man should have direct, unsupervised access to Kappa Leaguers until all clearances and training are confirmed.

The Five Kappa Men(tor) Fundamentals

Fundamental 1 — Be Friendly, Not a Friend▼

A mentor maintains a warm, supportive relationship while preserving appropriate professional boundaries. The goal is not to be a peer or buddy — it is to be a consistent, trustworthy adult figure. Avoid sharing personal problems, seeking validation from Kappa Leaguers, or creating relationships that blur the adult/youth dynamic.

Fundamental 2 — Be a Role Model▼

Kappa Men(tors) lead by example. What you do matters more than what you say. Model punctuality, professional dress, respectful communication, and civic engagement. Kappa Leaguers are always watching — your conduct on and off program grounds shapes their understanding of manhood.

Fundamental 3 — Accept Participants as They Are▼

Meet every Kappa Leaguer where he is — not where you wish he were. Some young men come from challenging home environments, have academic struggles, or carry social-emotional burdens. A good mentor creates an unconditionally supportive environment and works patiently toward growth, not perfection.

Fundamental 4 — Encourage School and Academic Achievement▼

Academic success is a cornerstone of Guide Right. Mentors should regularly ask about school performance, encourage consistent attendance, connect members with tutoring resources, and celebrate academic milestones. Challenge Kappa Leaguers to set and track GPA goals each semester.

Fundamental 5 — Set Goals Together▼

Effective mentors help Kappa Leaguers develop SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) goals across multiple life domains — academic, social, personal, and career. Regular goal check-ins create accountability and build the self-discipline needed for long-term success.

Establishing Trust

Trust is the foundation of any effective mentoring relationship. It cannot be demanded — it must be earned over time through consistency, honesty, and genuine care. Kappa Leaguers, especially those from under-resourced environments, may initially be guarded or skeptical of adult relationships.

Key Principle: Show up consistently. Be where you said you would be, when you said you would be there. Consistency is the fastest path to trust.

Mentor Connection: Getting to Know Your Mentee

Use these conversation starters in your first few one-on-one meetings. They come from the Kappa League Mentor Connection framework and are designed to help mentors understand the whole person — not just academic performance.

School Experience Questions▼
  • What do you enjoy most about school? What do you find most challenging?
  • Which subjects are you strongest in? Which need the most work?
  • How do you feel about your teachers? Do you feel supported at school?
  • What's your current GPA, and where would you like it to be?
Goals & Aspirations Questions▼
  • Where do you see yourself in 5 years? 10 years?
  • What career or profession are you most curious about?
  • What does success look like to you personally?
  • What college or training program are you considering after high school?
Strengths & Interests Questions▼
  • What are you really good at — inside or outside of school?
  • What activities or hobbies do you enjoy most?
  • Tell me about a time you accomplished something you were proud of.
  • Who are the people in your life who believe in you?
Challenges & Support Questions▼
  • What's the biggest challenge you're facing right now?
  • Is there anything happening at home or school that affects your ability to focus?
  • What kind of support would be most helpful to you?
  • What do you wish adults in your life better understood about you?
Gratitude & Connection Questions▼
  • What are three things in your life you're grateful for?
  • Who is someone who has made a positive difference in your life? How?
  • What does it mean to you to be part of Kappa League?

SMART Goal Setting

Guide your mentees to set goals that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. The SMART framework transforms vague aspirations into actionable plans.

MeasurableProgress can be tracked"How will you know when you've reached it?"
AchievableRealistic and attainable"Is this something you can realistically accomplish?"
RelevantAligned with broader goals"Why is this goal important to you?"

Sustaining the Relationship

Long-term mentoring requires intentional effort. After initial rapport is established, maintain momentum through consistent check-ins, celebrating small wins, and navigating setbacks with patience.

Feedback Best Practice: When giving feedback, use the "Sandwich" approach — start with a genuine strength, address the growth area, then close with encouragement. This method preserves dignity while promoting growth.

Social Media Policy for Mentors▼
  • Mentors must not connect with Kappa Leaguers on personal social media accounts (Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok, etc.)
  • All digital communication must occur through official chapter platforms or with full transparency to parents/guardians

Social Media Policy for Kappa Leaguers▼

Code of Conduct & Dress Code

Member Code of Conduct▼

Month 1 Spotlight: Communication Skills

The first month of Kappa League is designed to build foundational communication skills. The Month One Assignment focuses on a practical scenario that tests how young men respond in real-world communication challenges.

Month 1 Scenario: Navigating a Difficult Conversation▼

Scenario: A Kappa Leaguer has been experiencing conflict with a classmate who keeps disrupting his ability to focus during a group project. He wants to address it but isn't sure how to approach the conversation without it becoming an argument.

Assignment: Working with your mentor, develop a plan for how to approach this classmate respectfully and constructively. Role-play the conversation. Discuss how tone, body language, and word choice affect the outcome.

Learning Outcome: Kappa Leaguers practice assertive (not aggressive) communication, active listening, and conflict resolution — core social-emotional skills for academic and professional success.

Facilitating the Activity: Tips for Mentors▼
  • Set the tone: Create a non-judgmental space. Emphasize that there are no "wrong" answers in the exploration phase.
  • Model it first: Demonstrate the role-play yourself before asking the mentee to try.
  • Debrief after: Ask reflective questions — "How did that feel?" "What would you change?" "What worked well?"
  • Connect to their life: Ask the mentee to identify a real situation where these skills would be useful.
  • Document the session: Note key takeaways in the mentee's progress log for future reference.

Guide Right Certification (NGRC) Requirements

The National Guide Right Certification (NGRC) is awarded to chapters that demonstrate full compliance with program standards. Certification is required to be recognized as an official Kappa League chapter in good standing.

NGRC Certification Checklist▼
  • All active mentors have completed background checks (Tier 3)
  • All mentors have completed Youth Protection Training
  • All mentors have signed the Do's & Don'ts Acknowledgment
  • Chapter has a registered Guide Right Chairman on file
  • Annual Program Report submitted to national office
  • Community service logs completed and documented
  • Member enrollment forms and parental consent on file
  • NGRC online submission completed through the national portal
Program Sustainability Planning▼

Sustainable programs plan beyond a single year. Key sustainability strategies include:

  • Mentor Pipeline: Recruit new mentors annually; avoid single-point-of-failure dependency on one leader
  • Community Partnerships: Build relationships with schools, businesses, and nonprofits for resources and referrals
  • Parent Engagement: Active parent/guardian involvement increases retention and support
  • Documentation: Maintain thorough records so program knowledge transfers when leadership changes
  • Scholarship Support: Connect graduating Kappa Leaguers with scholarship resources to demonstrate long-term program value
