Course Overview

Course Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, all members will be able to demonstrate working knowledge of Kappa Alpha Psi's standards, governance, and operational expectations across six core areas.

Introduction Video
Watch this video before beginning the course modules.

This course is designed for all active members of the Denton-Lewisville chapter — whether newly initiated or long-standing brothers. It establishes a shared foundation so that every brother operates from the same understanding of fraternity standards. Completion of this course is expected of every active member.

Module 1 — Code of Conduct

Identify the fraternity's zero-tolerance hazing policy, define key terms, distinguish violation categories and severity levels, and describe the full disciplinary procedure from complaint through appeal.

Module 2 — Protocol & Etiquette

Apply proper traditions, forms of address for all officer levels, dress code standards by event type, badge and insignia rules, speaking and seating decorum, and written and electronic communication standards.

Module 3 — Risk Management

Explain the core liability principle, identify insurance coverage parameters, describe the event registration process, apply the Event Safety Checklist, and state policies on alcohol, drugs, and consent.

Module 4 — Conducting Meetings

Differentiate between province and national meeting types, explain the delegate's role and voting structure, describe standard meeting activities and expectations, and identify available leadership development programs.

Module 5 — Committee Structure

Define the three committee types, explain when each is used, identify core Standing Committees and their functions, describe committee reporting obligations, and list the responsibilities of committee members and chairs.

Module 6 — Organizational Chart

Navigate the full organizational hierarchy from Grand Chapter to General Body, identify the duties of local chapter officers, explain the Board of Directors' role, and describe how liaisons connect the chapter to external bodies.

Module 1 of 6

Code of Conduct

The standards that govern member behavior, the fraternity's absolute prohibition on hazing, the categories and severity levels of misconduct, and the structured disciplinary process that protects every brother's right to due process.

Module Learning Objectives

  • State the fraternity's zero-tolerance position on hazing and identify the MTA as the sole approved intake process.
  • Define at least eight key terms used in the Code of Conduct.
  • Distinguish between physical hazing, non-physical hazing, and conduct unbecoming a brother.
  • Identify the three levels of Conduct Unbecoming, their sanction ranges, and two examples of each.
  • Explain Chapter Misconduct and how it differs from individual member violations.
  • Describe each step of the fraternity's disciplinary procedure, including the accused member's rights.
  • State the member's affirmative duty to report violations and the consequences of remaining silent.

Foundation and Zero-Tolerance Policy

Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc. was founded on January 5, 1911, at Indiana University, Bloomington, Indiana. The ten Founders established the organization on principles of achievement, brotherhood, and leadership — principles that require every member to maintain the highest standards of personal and organizational conduct.

The fraternity maintains an absolute zero-tolerance policy for hazing in any form. There are no exceptions based on tradition, chapter custom, fraternal secrecy, or member consent. Any activity that endangers the physical or mental health of a candidate or member falls within this prohibition, regardless of how the activity is framed or who authorizes it.

The Membership Training Activity (MTA) is the only approved intake process in Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc. No chapter, province, or individual has the authority to deviate from the MTA framework. Unauthorized intake activities — no matter how long they have been practiced by a particular chapter — are violations of national policy and subject all participants to immediate disciplinary action including expulsion.

Key Definitions

The Code of Conduct uses specific defined terms. Brothers must know these terms to properly understand their rights, obligations, and exposure under the Code.

Applicant

Any person who has submitted interest paperwork but has not yet been formally accepted into the MTA process. An applicant has no membership rights or standing within the fraternity.

Candidate

A person who has been formally approved by the chapter and Grand Chapter to participate in the MTA. Candidates are under the protection of fraternity policy and may not be subjected to hazing.

Brother

Any duly initiated member of Kappa Alpha Psi in good standing, meaning all dues and financial obligations are current and no administrative holds are active.

Barred

A person permanently and irrevocably prohibited from membership in Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc. A barred individual may not participate in any fraternity activities in any capacity.

Code of Silence

Knowingly withholding information about a hazing incident or Code violation. The Code of Silence is itself a separate and distinct violation — not simply a moral failure. Members who remain silent may face the same sanctions as those who committed the underlying act.

Expulsion

The permanent removal of a member from Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc. An expelled member loses all rights, privileges, and recognitions of membership and may be declared Barred.

Suspension

A temporary removal from membership rights and privileges for a defined period. A suspended member may not vote, hold office, attend closed events, or represent the fraternity during the suspension period.

Reprimand

A formal written notice of violation placed in a member's record. A reprimand is the least severe sanction but is still an official disciplinary action and may be considered in any future disciplinary proceedings.

Cease & Desist

A formal order requiring a member or chapter to immediately stop a prohibited activity. Failure to comply with a Cease & Desist elevates the severity of the violation and may result in suspension or expulsion.

Administrative Hold

A freeze placed on a member's record that prevents them from voting, being recognized, or holding office. Common reasons include unpaid dues, outstanding fines, or pending disciplinary action.

Slush Funds

Unofficial money collected outside approved chapter financial accounts. Slush funds create personal financial liability for those who collect or distribute them and are prohibited regardless of the intended purpose.

Probation

A period during which a member or chapter operates under heightened oversight. Violations during probation are treated with greater severity, and probation may include conditions such as mandatory training or restricted event participation.

Section I — MTA Violations

Section I addresses any deviation from the approved Membership Training Activity process. The MTA is a nationally standardized, time-limited program with a defined curriculum, timeline, and participant list. Any of the following constitute MTA violations:

  • Conducting activities outside the approved MTA timeline or curriculum
  • Allowing unauthorized persons — brothers or non-members — to participate in or observe the MTA process
  • Collecting fees, gifts, or contributions from candidates beyond those explicitly authorized in the MTA guidelines
  • Conducting a "shadow" or parallel intake process alongside or in addition to the official MTA
  • Initiating a member without completing the full MTA process
  • Permitting a suspended, barred, or non-member to serve in any capacity during the MTA
MTA violations are reported to the Province Polemarch and Grand Chapter simultaneously. They may result in chapter suspension, loss of the chapter's intake privileges, and individual expulsion of all participating brothers.

Section II — Hazing

Hazing is defined as any action, activity, or situation — whether on or off fraternity premises — that recklessly or intentionally endangers the physical health, mental health, or dignity of a member, candidate, or applicant, regardless of the person's consent or willingness to participate.

TypeDefinitionExamples
Physical HazingAny act causing or risking physical harm or discomfortPaddling or striking; forcing physical exercise to the point of exhaustion; sleep deprivation; food deprivation; exposure to extreme temperatures; forced consumption of food, alcohol, or substances
Non-Physical HazingAny act causing psychological harm, humiliation, or coercive distressVerbal abuse and degradation; forced memorization under threat of punishment; financial exploitation; isolation from family or friends; public humiliation rituals; forced servitude or degrading tasks

Consent is not a defense. The fraternity's position is that a candidate's environment — the power imbalance between initiates and initiated members — makes truly free consent impossible during the intake process. Any claimed consent is therefore not a mitigating factor.

Reporting Obligation: Every brother has an affirmative duty to report hazing. Reporting to the chapter Polemarch, Province Polemarch, or Grand Chapter directly are all acceptable channels. Choosing not to report — even if the brother did not participate — constitutes a Code of Silence violation and carries the same potential sanctions as participation.

Section III — Conduct Unbecoming

Conduct Unbecoming refers to any behavior by a brother that reflects negatively on himself, the chapter, or the fraternity, or that violates the fraternity's values and standards outside the specific context of hazing or intake. The Code organizes these violations into three levels of increasing severity.

Level 1 Minor Violations

Level 1 violations are addressed and resolved at the chapter level by the chapter officers. They do not require province or national involvement unless they recur or are left unaddressed. Examples include:

  • Repeated unexcused absences from chapter meetings without notification
  • Failure to pay dues or meet financial obligations to the chapter within the allotted period
  • Disrespectful or dismissive behavior toward chapter officers during official meetings
  • Failure to fulfill an assigned committee responsibility without notifying the chair
  • Wearing the badge, letters, or insignia at an event that conflicts with fraternity values

Typical sanctions: Formal reprimand; requirement to complete a training module; temporary suspension of voting rights at the chapter level.

Level 2 Moderate Violations

Level 2 violations require involvement from the Province Polemarch and Province Board. They reflect behavior that embarrasses the fraternity in the community or demonstrates a pattern of disregard for fraternity standards. Examples include:

  • Public intoxication while wearing fraternity insignia or representing the chapter at an event
  • Misuse, misappropriation, or unauthorized disbursement of chapter funds
  • Making false or defamatory statements about a brother or chapter officer
  • Conduct at a fraternity event that results in legal involvement, a complaint to the host institution, or adverse media attention
  • Repeated Level 1 violations that have not been corrected after formal reprimand

Typical sanctions: Suspension from chapter activities; financial restitution; mandatory province-level training; probation with conditions.

Level 3 Severe Violations

Level 3 violations are handled directly by the Grand Chapter. They represent behavior so serious that they threaten the integrity, legal standing, or public reputation of the fraternity at a national level. Examples include:

  • Any criminal conviction that reflects directly on the fraternity's values
  • Sexual misconduct or assault involving any fraternity event, member, or use of the fraternity's name
  • Participation in, facilitation of, or failure to report hazing (including Code of Silence violations)
  • Falsification of official fraternity records, intake documents, or financial reports
  • Operating an unauthorized chapter or engaging in fraternity activities after suspension or expulsion

Typical sanctions: Immediate suspension pending hearing; expulsion; declaration as Barred; referral to law enforcement where applicable.

Section IV — Chapter Misconduct

The Code recognizes that violations are not always the act of a single individual. A chapter itself can be found in violation when the organization collectively enables, condones, or fails to prevent prohibited behavior. Chapter Misconduct findings can result in suspension of the chapter's charter, loss of intake privileges, required supervision by the Province, or dissolution.

Specific grounds for a Chapter Misconduct finding include:

  • Failure to maintain accurate membership, financial, or intake records as required by national policy
  • Conducting an unauthorized event that results in harm to attendees
  • Allowing members in bad standing, suspended individuals, or non-members to participate in official chapter business
  • Pattern of unresolved individual violations that the chapter leadership has failed to address
  • Operating slush funds or conducting chapter business through unofficial financial channels

Disciplinary Procedure (Sections A–T)

The Code of Conduct establishes a comprehensive, multi-step disciplinary procedure designed to ensure fairness, transparency, and due process for all parties. The level at which each step occurs — chapter, province, or Grand Chapter — depends on the severity of the alleged violation.

  • AComplaint Filed: Any member, candidate, or affected party may file a formal written complaint with the chapter Polemarch, Province Polemarch, or Grand Chapter, depending on the severity of the alleged violation.
  • BPreliminary Review: The receiving officer reviews the complaint to determine if it states a cognizable violation and whether it falls within their jurisdiction. Frivolous or anonymous complaints may be dismissed at this stage.
  • CInvestigation: A designated investigator or committee conducts a fact-finding inquiry. The accused is not required to cooperate but the investigation proceeds regardless.
  • DFormal Notice: The accused member receives written notice of the complaint, the specific charges, the evidence gathered, and the scheduled hearing date. The accused is entitled to reasonable time to prepare a response.
  • ERight to Respond: The accused may submit a written response to the charges prior to the hearing. The accused may also be accompanied by a fraternity member of their choosing at the hearing.
  • FHearing: A formal hearing is conducted before the appropriate body. Both the complainant and the accused may present evidence and witnesses. The hearing is not a legal proceeding but follows orderly procedure.
  • GDecision and Sanction: The hearing body deliberates and renders a decision. If a violation is found, the body recommends a sanction appropriate to the severity of the offense and the brother's history.
  • HAppeal: The accused member has the right to appeal a finding to the next higher body within the specified timeframe (typically 30 days). The appeal must state specific grounds — procedural error, new evidence, or disproportionate sanction.
  • IFinal Action: Upon exhaustion of appeals or expiration of the appeal period, the sanction is enforced. Enforcement may include administrative holds, formal notification to institutions, or public fraternity records updates.

Module 2 of 6

Protocol & Etiquette

The traditions, forms of address, dress standards, badge and insignia rules, meeting decorum, and communication protocols that define how Kappa Alpha Psi members present themselves in every fraternal and public setting.

Module Learning Objectives

  • Name the six fraternity traditions and explain the practical expectation each places on members.
  • Apply the correct form of address for national, province, and chapter officers, and for non-officer brothers.
  • State the appropriate dress code for standard meetings, formal banquets, and Founders Day programs.
  • Describe the correct placement and order of precedence for wearing the badge, Life Membership Pin, and other insignia.
  • Demonstrate proper speaking decorum — introduction, procedure, and behavior at the microphone.
  • Apply seating protocol for Grand Board and Province Board members at official programs.
  • Distinguish between appropriate and prohibited uses of electronic communication for fraternity matters.

The Six Fraternity Traditions

These traditions are not historical artifacts — they are active expectations that shape how every brother conducts himself within and outside the fraternity.

Brotherhood

The bond between members extends beyond the local chapter. Brothers are expected to recognize, greet, and extend courtesy to members from any chapter. Brotherhood is demonstrated through active participation, mutual support, and genuine investment in other brothers' success.

Self-Discipline

Brothers are held to a higher standard of personal conduct. Self-discipline means controlling one's speech, appearance, and behavior — especially in public settings — even when no one from the fraternity is watching. Character is consistent.

Intra-Fraternity Relations

Chapters within Kappa Alpha Psi must treat one another with respect and collegiality. Disputes between chapters are resolved through proper channels, not public confrontation. Brothers support the success of other chapters as an expression of organizational pride.

Extra-Fraternity Relations

How Kappas interact with other Greek organizations and the general public reflects on the entire fraternity. Brothers are expected to be courteous, collaborative, and professional in all inter-organizational settings. Conduct that embarrasses the fraternity in relation to another organization is a Code violation.

Achievement & Leadership

The fraternity's motto — "Achievement in Every Field of Human Endeavor" — is not aspirational. It is an obligation. Brothers are expected to actively pursue excellence in their careers, education, and community contributions. Mediocrity is inconsistent with membership.

General Fraternity Practices

Brothers adhere to all established rituals, customs, and protocols as defined by Grand Chapter. This includes proper conduct at all fraternity events, adherence to meeting procedures, and respect for the sacred elements of the fraternity's history and traditions.

Dress Code by Event Type

Personal appearance is a direct reflection of the fraternity. The following standards are mandatory — not suggested — for the events indicated.

Standard Fraternity Business Events

Kappa Blazer, white dress shirt, and official Kappa necktie. Shirt must be pressed and collar buttoned. Slacks and dress shoes. No athletic wear, t-shirts, or casual footwear.

Closed Banquet / Province or Grand Chapter Banquet

Black-tie tuxedo, required without exception. Fraternity pin worn over the heart. White formal shirt, bow tie, and dress shoes appropriate to the occasion.

Founders Day Program

Formal attire. National Founders Day in January requires tuxedo. Local chapter Founders Day observances follow the same formal standard unless the local chapter has formally voted to vary the dress code for a particular event portion.

Province Council Meeting — Business Sessions

Kappa Blazer with dress shirt and Kappa tie. This applies to all official business meeting sessions regardless of the time of day or informality of the agenda item.

Public Meeting / Community Events

Kappa Blazer and tie as the standard. Brothers must present a unified, professional appearance when representing the fraternity to the public or to other organizations.

Casual/Social Events

Unless specifically designated as formal, fraternity social events allow business casual attire. However, if Kappa insignia is worn, the overall appearance must reflect positively on the fraternity at all times.

Badge, Pins, and Insignia

The badge is the most sacred emblem of membership in Kappa Alpha Psi. It must be worn with reverence and in strict accordance with fraternity guidelines.

  • The badge is worn over the heart — on the left chest. It is never worn on the right side, below the waist, or on non-fraternity clothing.
  • The order of precedence when multiple insignia are worn: Badge → Life Membership Pin → 50-Year Pin → 25-Year Pin → Medallion.
  • Insignia is never worn at events that conflict with fraternity values, including events involving illegal activity, events that demean the fraternity's image, or events where the brother's conduct does not meet fraternity standards.
  • Renting, lending, or selling the badge or any official insignia to a non-member is a serious Code violation.
  • The badge must be secured properly at all times. Losing a badge should be reported promptly and a replacement obtained through official channels.

Speaking and Seating Decorum

Speaking at Official Programs and Meetings:

  • Always stand before speaking. A brother does not speak from a seated position during any formal meeting or program.
  • If a microphone is available, approach it before speaking. Do not speak past the microphone from your seat.
  • Introduce yourself each time you speak: "Brother [Last Name], [Chapter Name], [Province]." This applies even if you have spoken earlier in the session.
  • Address remarks to the presiding officer, not to other brothers directly. In a meeting: "Brother Polemarch, I move that…" not "I think we should…"
  • Do not interrupt another brother while he is speaking. Wait to be recognized by the presiding officer.

Seating at Programs:

  • The Grand Board of Directors is always seated on the dais at national programs. No other brothers take seats on the dais unless they hold an appropriate office for that program.
  • The Province Board of Directors is seated on the dais at province-level meetings and programs.
  • Local chapter officers may be designated reserved seating near the front at local events, but this should be formally established in the event program.
  • Brothers should be in their seats before the program begins. Late arrivals should enter quietly and take available seating without disrupting the program.

Fraternity Communication Standards

Verbal Communication

Officers are addressed by title in all formal settings. General brothers are addressed as "Brother [Last Name]." Using a brother's first name alone in a formal setting is a breach of protocol. When addressing a dignitary, always use the full title before the name.

Written Correspondence

All official written communications from or on behalf of the chapter must follow formal letter format:

  • Address the recipient by full title and name on the salutation line
  • State the fraternal year, chapter name, and province in the letterhead or opening line
  • Use formal language throughout — avoid colloquialisms and abbreviations
  • Close all formal letters with: "Yours in the Bond," followed by the writer's full name, title, chapter name, and province
  • Official chapter correspondence must be reviewed and signed by the Keeper of Records & Exchequer and the Polemarch before being sent

Electronic Communication

Prohibited: Electronic mail, text messaging, social media platforms, group chats, and any digital medium must never be used to communicate esoteric matters, ritual content, confidential fraternity business, or any information that could identify candidates during the intake period. These matters must be handled in person or through secured, physical written correspondence only. Brothers who transmit protected fraternity content electronically may face immediate disciplinary action.

Electronic communication is appropriate for: meeting reminders and logistics, sharing publicly available chapter news, coordinating service project details, and communicating event information that contains no sensitive fraternity content.

Module 3 of 6

Risk Management

Understanding fraternity liability, the parameters of insurance coverage, the mandatory event registration process, the complete Event Safety Checklist, and the chapter's obligations regarding alcohol, drugs, and member safety.

Module Learning Objectives

  • State the core principle of risk management and explain why liability cannot be fully eliminated.
  • Identify what the $1,000,000 blanket liability policy covers and what it explicitly excludes.
  • Describe the KappaOrg event registration requirement including the 2-week advance deadline.
  • Define all eight key risk management terms: Host Chapter, Event, Party, Planned, Sponsored, Hosted, Promoted, Slush Funds, Pregame, Drinking Games.
  • Apply each item on the Event Safety Checklist and explain why it reduces chapter exposure.
  • State the chapter's specific obligations regarding alcohol service, drug use, and consent at any affiliated event.
  • Describe the personal liability a brother assumes when he collects or distributes slush funds.

The Core Principle

"You can never get rid of liability — you can only manage it."

Every action the chapter takes — hosting an event, promoting a gathering, co-sponsoring an activity — creates some level of legal and financial risk. The goal of the fraternity's risk management framework is not to eliminate all risk, which is impossible, but to reduce foreseeable risks to a manageable level and to ensure that when something does go wrong, the chapter has followed every reasonable precaution.

Failure to follow risk management protocols does not just expose the chapter to liability. It exposes individual brothers personally — their personal assets, academic standing, and employment can all be affected by incidents at improperly managed chapter events. Risk management is self-protection.

Fraternity Insurance Coverage

Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc. provides a $1,000,000 blanket general liability policy for registered chapter events. This policy provides the chapter's first line of financial defense in the event of a claim arising from an officially registered event.

Covered Under Fraternity InsuranceNOT Covered — No Protection
Active brothers in good financial standing (dues current, no administrative hold)Brothers with unpaid dues or active administrative holds
Events registered on KappaOrg at least 2 weeks in advanceUnregistered events or events registered late
Activities conducted within the scope of the approved eventIndividual acts that fall outside the approved event parameters
Chapter-sponsored community service activitiesEvents involving alcohol law violations, hazing, or other Code violations
Guests present at the registered event within the documented guest listUninvited individuals or those admitted after the guest list was closed
Key implication: If a brother is not in good standing on the date of an incident, the fraternity's insurance does not cover him. If the event was not registered, the fraternity's insurance does not apply. In both cases, the individual brother and chapter officers may face personal civil or criminal liability.

Event Registration Requirements

Registration is not optional. The Constitution and Statutes require all chapter events to be formally registered through the official fraternity portal.

  • Platform: All events must be registered on KappaOrg — the fraternity's official national membership and event management portal.
  • Deadline: Registration must be submitted and approved at least two (2) weeks before the event date. Submitting on the deadline date does not guarantee approval.
  • Responsibility: The chapter Polemarch and the Risk Management Chairman share responsibility for ensuring timely event registration. This cannot be delegated to a member without an official chapter office.
  • Late Registration: Events registered after the deadline may still be submitted but will not receive insurance coverage. The Province Polemarch must also be notified of the late registration.
  • Post-Event Reporting: Certain events require a post-event report to be filed on KappaOrg within 30 days, documenting attendance, incidents (if any), and event outcomes.

Key Risk Management Definitions

Host Chapter

The chapter that assumes legal and financial responsibility for an event. Being the Host Chapter means accepting liability for everything that happens at the event, whether or not the chapter planned it.

Event / Party

Any gathering, activity, or function — formal or informal — organized, sponsored, hosted, or promoted by the chapter or by identifiable chapter members acting in a fraternal capacity.

Sponsored

The chapter is the primary financial backer of the event. Sponsorship creates the highest level of liability exposure because the chapter is directly funding the activity.

Hosted

The chapter provides the venue or primary organizational infrastructure. Even if another entity funds the event, hosting creates significant liability for what occurs on the chapter's premises.

Promoted

The chapter advertises, publicizes, or otherwise encourages attendance at an event. Critically, promotion creates liability exposure even if the chapter did not organize or fund the event. If the chapter's name or letters appear on a flyer for an event where harm occurs, the chapter may be held liable.

Slush Funds

Unofficial money collected, held, or distributed outside the chapter's official financial accounts. Slush funds bypass the Treasurer and official budget process. Any brother who collects or distributes slush funds assumes full personal financial and legal liability for those funds.

Pregame

Drinking or informal gatherings that occur before an official chapter event. Even if the pregame is not organized by the chapter, brothers who participate and then attend the official event may extend the chapter's liability window. The fraternity considers the pregame part of the event's liability exposure.

Drinking Games

Any structured activity designed to promote or reward rapid alcohol consumption. Drinking games are strictly prohibited at any event affiliated with the chapter, regardless of whether the event is registered. Their presence at an event is automatic grounds for the insurance policy to deny coverage for any resulting claim.

Event Safety Checklist

Every registered event must be reviewed against this checklist by the Risk Management Chairman prior to the event date. If any item cannot be confirmed, the event should be postponed until compliance is achieved.

  • 1Registered On Time: Confirm the event has been submitted and approved on KappaOrg at least two weeks prior. Print or screenshot the confirmation for chapter records. Without this, there is no insurance coverage.
  • 2Guest List Maintained: A written list of all invited and expected guests must be prepared before the event and checked at the door. Walk-in guests without prior authorization should not be admitted. The guest list is legal documentation in the event of an incident.
  • 3Safe Rides Arranged: Transportation options must be communicated to all attendees in advance. Post rideshare codes, taxi numbers, or designated driver arrangements. No brother or guest should be driving impaired from a chapter event.
  • 4Substantive Food Available: If alcohol is served, unsalted, substantive food must be available throughout the event — not just at the beginning. Food that slows alcohol absorption is a legal requirement in many jurisdictions and directly reduces liability exposure.
  • 5ID Checked at Entry: All attendees must present valid, government-issued identification before entry to any event where alcohol is present. No exceptions. A brother who allows a minor to access alcohol faces personal criminal liability under state law.
  • 6Designated Sober Brother: At least one brother must be formally assigned as the Sober Brother for the event. The Sober Brother does not consume alcohol, monitors the event for safety concerns, intervenes in problem situations, and ensures the event ends safely. This role must be assigned before the event, not improvised during it.
  • 7No Slush Funds: Confirm that all event funding has been processed through official chapter accounts and approved by the Treasurer and chapter vote. No money should be collected at the door or from attendees in an unofficial capacity.
  • 8No Drinking Games: Confirm that no organized drinking games, drinking challenges, or consumption competitions are planned or permitted at the event in any form.

Alcohol and Drug Policy

Alcohol: Alcohol may be present at a chapter event only when the event is properly registered, the venue is legally permitted to have alcohol, and all attendees consuming alcohol are verified to be of legal drinking age. The chapter may not purchase alcohol for the event using chapter funds in most circumstances — consult the fraternity's current event guidelines for the specific rules governing alcohol funding. Brothers who provide alcohol to minors face criminal prosecution independent of any fraternity disciplinary action.

Illegal Drugs: The presence or use of illegal drugs at any event affiliated with the chapter — in any quantity, by any person — is an automatic basis for the insurance policy to deny all coverage and for all chapter officers to face disciplinary action. There are no exceptions. Brothers found to have facilitated or knowingly permitted drug use at a chapter event face Level 3 disciplinary action.

Sexual Assault, Harassment, and Consent

The fraternity has an absolute zero-tolerance policy for sexual assault and harassment at any event or in any context where brothers are acting in a fraternal capacity. This policy applies regardless of the relationship between the parties involved.

Consent must be:

  • Freely given — not obtained through pressure, manipulation, or coercion
  • Reversible — anyone can change their mind at any point
  • Informed — both parties understand what they are agreeing to
  • Enthusiastic — the absence of a "no" is not a "yes"
  • Sober — a person who is intoxicated cannot legally give consent

Bystander Obligation: Brothers who witness behavior that appears threatening, non-consensual, or harassing are required to intervene. Acceptable intervention includes approaching the individuals directly, alerting the Sober Brother or event organizer, or contacting security. A brother who witnesses potential sexual misconduct and does nothing may face disciplinary action for failure to act.

Any incident of alleged sexual assault or harassment at a chapter event must be reported to law enforcement and to the Province Polemarch. The chapter may not attempt to resolve such allegations internally. Failure to report such an incident to the appropriate authorities is a Level 3 Code violation.

Conducting Meetings & Meeting Expectations

The structure and purpose of business meetings at every level of the fraternity, the delegate's role and voting rights, standard meeting activities, parliamentary conduct expectations, and the training programs that develop Kappa's leaders.

Types of Business Meetings

Kappa Alpha Psi conducts formal business at multiple levels. Understanding which meeting does what — and what is expected of the chapter at each — is a foundational responsibility of every member.

Province Council Meeting

Frequency: Annual. All chapters within the province gather to conduct official business, elect province officers, recognize chapter achievements, and engage with the local community. The host chapter rotates alphabetically among chapters in the province. Each chapter is required by the Constitution and Statutes to send a Delegate.

Grand Chapter Meeting

Frequency: Bi-annual (every two years). The national business meeting of the fraternity. Chapters from across the country gather to conduct national business, amend the Constitution, elect national officers, and recognize the fraternity's highest achievers. Host chapters — both undergraduate and alumni — submit formal bids for the right to host.

Undergraduate Summit

Frequency: Annual, within each Province. A meeting organized and led entirely by undergraduate brothers. Purpose: open dialogue on challenges facing undergraduate chapters, goal-setting, and strengthening connections between undergraduate chapters across the province. Alumni attend only in an advisory, non-voting capacity.

National Founders Day

Frequency: Annual. Celebrates the founding of the fraternity on January 5, 1911. A host chapter is selected annually. Activities include community service, social events, and a Closed Banquet featuring the Oath of Rededication. Brothers from across the fraternity attend to renew their commitment to Kappa's founding principles.

The Chapter Delegate

The Delegate is the chapter's official representative at Province Council and Grand Chapter meetings. The role carries both privilege and responsibility.

Requirements: The Delegate must be an active member of the chapter in good standing — all dues paid, no administrative holds, no active suspension. The chapter Polemarch formally designates the Delegate prior to the meeting and communicates this designation to the Province.

Voting Rights: Every chapter, regardless of size, is allotted exactly three (3) votes at Province Council and Grand Chapter meetings. This equal representation structure ensures that small chapters have the same institutional voice as large chapters. The Delegate casts all three votes on behalf of the chapter.

Responsibilities of the Delegate:

  • Attend all scheduled business sessions of the meeting — unexcused absence forfeits the chapter's votes
  • Carry the chapter's formal positions on pending motions and votes, as determined by a chapter vote before the meeting
  • Conduct themselves in full compliance with Protocol and Etiquette standards throughout the meeting
  • Take detailed notes of all actions taken at the meeting and report back to the full chapter within a reasonable period after returning
  • Collect and return all meeting materials, official publications, and communications distributed at the meeting
Constitutional Requirement: The Constitution and Statutes require each chapter to send a Delegate to both Province Council and Grand Chapter meetings. A chapter that repeatedly fails to send a Delegate may face sanctions including loss of voting rights and, ultimately, suspension of the chapter's charter.

Business Meeting Activities

Province Council and Grand Chapter meetings follow a structured schedule. Brothers attending in any capacity should understand what each component entails and what conduct is expected.

  • 1Public Meeting: An open session welcoming community members and guests to observe formal fraternity business. The chapter demonstrates its commitment to transparency and community engagement. Brothers must be in full protocol dress and on their best behavior during public sessions.
  • 2Undergraduate Luncheon: A dedicated gathering for undergraduate brothers to network, discuss challenges unique to their chapters, and build relationships across chapters. The Province Polemarch or a designated facilitator leads the discussion.
  • 3Business Sessions: Formal meetings where motions are made, debated, and voted upon. All business is conducted according to parliamentary procedure (Robert's Rules of Order). Delegates vote; observers may not interrupt or participate in voting. The presiding officer controls the floor.
  • 4Awards Ceremony: Outstanding brothers, chapters, and community leaders are formally recognized. Both Province Council and Grand Chapter present a series of awards. Attendance is expected of all brothers attending the meeting.
  • 5Closed Banquet: A formal, tuxedo-required event open to brothers only. The Closed Banquet includes the Oath of Rededication, in which brothers formally renew their commitment to the fraternity's founding principles. Conduct and protocol expectations are at their highest during the Closed Banquet.
  • 6Elections of Leadership: Province and national officers are elected by eligible Delegates. Candidates must meet specific requirements including active status and service history. Elections must be conducted fairly, transparently, and in accordance with the Constitution — any interference with a fair election is a Code violation.

Meeting Conduct Expectations

The following standards apply at all formal fraternity meetings, whether at the local chapter level, province, or national.

As an Attendee

  • Arrive on time. Late arrivals disrupt quorum counts, interrupt proceedings, and demonstrate disrespect for the body. If you will be late, notify the Polemarch or chapter Secretary in advance.
  • Silence all electronic devices before the meeting is called to order. Texting, browsing, or taking calls during a session is a breach of protocol.
  • Maintain appropriate dress for the event. Do not attend formal sessions in casual attire.
  • Maintain confidentiality. Closed-session discussions are confidential. Nothing said in a closed session may be shared outside the meeting without the presiding officer's authorization.

As a Speaking Participant

  • Rise and wait to be recognized by the presiding officer before speaking on any matter.
  • Identify yourself fully: name, chapter, and province at Province Council and Grand Chapter; name and role at local meetings.
  • Speak to the motion or agenda item — do not introduce unrelated topics during debate on a motion.
  • Debate the issue, not the person. Personal attacks on other brothers during a business session are out of order and subject to being ruled a breach of conduct.
  • Accept the outcome of a properly conducted vote. A brother who refuses to accept a democratic outcome may be ruled out of order and removed from the session.

Committee Structure

The three types of committees that drive chapter operations — Standing, Ad Hoc, and Special — their distinct purposes, authority levels, reporting obligations, and the responsibilities every committee member carries.

The Role of Committees in Chapter Governance

Committees are the primary operational units of a well-functioning chapter. They exist because the full General Body cannot efficiently deliberate on every detail of chapter operations. Committees research, plan, and execute within their assigned areas, then bring their work products and recommendations back to the full membership for approval and vote.

This structure serves two critical purposes. First, it distributes work across a broader portion of the membership, preventing burnout in officers and building engagement across all brothers. Second, it maintains accountability — no committee acts unilaterally on behalf of the chapter without the General Body's authorization.

Core principle: Committees propose and execute. The General Body decides. A committee that takes action beyond its authorized scope has overstepped, and the action may be void or subject to reversal by a chapter vote.

Every active brother is expected to serve on at least one committee. Service on a committee is not optional for members in good standing. Brothers who decline all committee service without cause may be considered inactive for chapter purposes.

Standing Committees

Standing Committees are permanent committees established by the chapter's Constitution and Bylaws. They exist from year to year regardless of who serves on them. Because the work of a Standing Committee never ends — the chapter always needs to manage its finances, develop its youth program, and manage risk — these committees are structural features of the chapter, not temporary responses to a specific need.

Characteristics of Standing Committees:

  • Authorized by name in the chapter's Constitution and Bylaws
  • Operate continuously throughout the fraternal year, from installation of officers to end of term
  • Chaired by a member appointed by the Polemarch or elected by the General Body, as specified in the Bylaws
  • Report formally to the General Body at every regular chapter meeting — even if the report is only "no update this period"
  • Maintain ongoing records, minutes of committee meetings, and documentation of all major decisions and expenditures

Core Standing Committees:

Finance Committee

Oversees the annual chapter budget, reviews all proposed expenditures above the Treasurer's spending authority, audits financial records, and ensures that all fraternity funds are held and disbursed through official accounts. Works directly with the Keeper of Records & Exchequer.

Guide Right / Youth Development

Plans and executes the chapter's youth mentoring programs. Manages Kappa Kamp participation, coordinates with local schools and youth organizations, and tracks program outcomes. This committee directly fulfills the fraternity's primary service mission.

Membership Committee

Manages the MTA intake process from applicant tracking through candidate orientation. Oversees member retention and reclamation efforts. Maintains the official chapter membership roll. Works with the Province to ensure intake compliance.

Risk Management Committee

Reviews all upcoming events for compliance, leads the Event Safety Checklist review before every registered event, manages insurance documentation, and enforces the chapter's risk management policies. The Risk Management Chairman reports directly to the Polemarch on all risk-related matters.

Social Action / Community Service

Plans the chapter's community engagement calendar, coordinates volunteer activities, builds partnerships with local nonprofits and community organizations, and tracks service hours for reporting to Grand Chapter. This committee is responsible for the chapter's public-facing community identity.

Protocol & Scholarship

Ensures that all brothers receive and maintain proper training in fraternity protocol and etiquette. Manages scholarship opportunities for chapter members and community youth. Tracks academic achievement among active brothers and recognizes academic excellence.

Ad Hoc Committees

Ad Hoc Committees are temporary committees created to address a specific, defined need. They are not in the Bylaws because they are not permanent features of the chapter — they are responses to a particular situation or project.

Characteristics of Ad Hoc Committees:

  • Created by a motion and vote of the General Body, or at the direction of the Polemarch for administrative matters within the Polemarch's authority
  • Given a specific, written charge — the task the committee is created to accomplish — at the time of their formation
  • Limited in authority strictly to their written charge; they may not expand their scope without a new General Body vote
  • Dissolved automatically when their final report or work product is submitted to the General Body and accepted
  • Do not carry over from one fraternal year to the next unless a new vote re-establishes them

Examples of Ad Hoc Committees:

  • Charter Anniversary Planning Committee (tasked with planning a single event)
  • Bylaws Revision Committee (tasked with proposing amendments for a General Body vote)
  • Venue Selection Committee (tasked with identifying and recommending a location for a specific event)
  • Grievance Investigation Committee (tasked with investigating a specific complaint and reporting findings)
  • Social Media Policy Committee (tasked with drafting a chapter social media policy for General Body adoption)
Key distinction: An Ad Hoc Committee does not have independent authority to implement its recommendations. It researches, proposes, and reports — then the General Body decides whether to adopt the recommendation. If the committee acts on its own without General Body approval, those actions have no binding effect on the chapter.

Special Committees

Special Committees are committees of elevated importance or sensitivity, appointed when a task requires broader authority, higher-level oversight, or involvement that extends beyond the local chapter. They are a distinct category from standard Ad Hoc Committees.

Characteristics of Special Committees:

  • May be appointed at the chapter, province, or Grand Chapter level — the level of appointment determines the level of oversight
  • Typically handle matters of significant organizational sensitivity: disciplinary appeals, constitutional review, strategic planning, or investigations that cannot be handled at the chapter level
  • May be granted broader authority than a standard Ad Hoc Committee, as explicitly stated in the resolution or order that creates them
  • May include members from outside the local chapter — for example, a province-appointed oversight committee placed on a chapter under probation
  • Like Ad Hoc Committees, they are dissolved once their work is complete; unlike Ad Hoc Committees, their dissolution may require formal action by the appointing body rather than automatic dissolution

Examples of Special Committees:

  • Judicial Appeals Committee (hearing a member's formal appeal of a disciplinary decision)
  • Strategic Vision Committee (developing a multi-year plan for presentation to Grand Chapter)
  • Province Oversight Committee (placed on a chapter in conflict or under probation)
  • National Task Force (established by Grand Chapter to address a fraternity-wide policy issue)

Committee Chair Responsibilities

The committee chair is accountable to the General Body and to the chapter Polemarch for the work of the committee. The chair's responsibilities include:

  • Scheduling and facilitating all committee meetings; maintaining attendance records
  • Setting a written agenda for each committee meeting and distributing it to members in advance
  • Ensuring every committee meeting produces action items with assigned owners and deadlines
  • Preparing a written committee report for each General Body meeting; delivering the report verbally and in writing
  • Escalating to the Polemarch any matter that falls outside the committee's authority or requires General Body action
  • Maintaining a committee file containing meeting minutes, correspondence, financial records, and work products that can be passed to the next committee chair at the end of the fraternal year

Committee Member Obligations

  • Attend all committee meetings. Unexcused absences should be reported to the chair and may be reported to the Polemarch.
  • Carry out all assigned tasks within agreed-upon deadlines and communicate proactively if a deadline cannot be met.
  • Bring analysis and recommendations to the committee — not just problems. A committee member who only identifies obstacles without proposing solutions is not fulfilling the role.
  • Maintain confidentiality of committee deliberations where the committee is handling sensitive matters (personnel, disciplinary, financial).
  • Operate strictly within the committee's authorized scope. Do not take actions that bind the chapter or commit chapter resources without General Body approval.
  • Support the committee chair's reporting to the General Body and contribute to the committee's written records.

How Authority Flows in the Fraternity

Top-down policy: Grand Chapter establishes fraternity-wide standards, policies, and constitutional requirements. Province enforces and implements those standards within the province. Local chapters operate within the framework set by both Grand Chapter and Province.

Bottom-up accountability: The General Body holds local leadership accountable through regular elections and votes. Local leadership reports to Province. Province reports to Grand Chapter. Every level of the organization is accountable to the level above it.

Understanding where you stand in this structure — and who is accountable to whom — is essential for effective participation at every level of fraternity life.

Why Digital Standards Matter

Social media and digital platforms have become the primary way chapters communicate with the public, recruit prospective members, and demonstrate the fraternity’s impact in the community. Every post, photo, video, and web page associated with the Denton-Lewisville Chapter is a direct extension of the Kappa Alpha Psi brand and is subject to national fraternity standards.

Core assumption: Information sent over the internet is insecure and can eventually find its way into the public domain. This applies to “closed sites,” private groups, and direct messages as well. Brothers must assume that any digital content they create or share in a fraternal context may become public, regardless of privacy settings. When in doubt, do not post.

Social Media Defined

Social media encompasses the websites, applications, and mobile apps used for multimedia communication. The fraternity’s guidelines apply to all of the following categories:

Social Networking

Facebook, Instagram. Used for community building, event promotion, and member engagement. Chapter accounts must reflect the province or chapter they represent.

Professional Networking

LinkedIn. Used to promote brothers’ professional achievements and the fraternity’s community impact. Content must align with the fraternity’s professional image.

Video & Media Sharing

YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat, Vimeo, Facebook Live. All video content must comply with multimedia guidelines. No auto-play on websites.

Micro-Blogging

Twitter/X, Tumblr. Short-form content that reaches broad audiences quickly. Each post is a public-facing representation of the chapter and fraternity.

Chat & Messaging

Instagram DM, Signal. Even private messaging platforms are covered. Esoteric or confidential fraternity content may never be transmitted electronically under any circumstances.

Blogs & Online Content

WordPress, Tumblr. Long-form content platforms. All written content must meet the fraternity’s content, grammar, and accuracy standards.

Mandatory Social Media Rules

The following rules apply to every account and every post from any chapter, officer, or member of Kappa Alpha Psi in a fraternal capacity. Non-compliance with these rules is a conduct violation.

  • 1Represent the chapter or entity: The account must be a clear reflection of the province, chapter, or event it represents. Personal social media accounts may not claim to represent the chapter without authorization from the Polemarch and Board of Directors.
  • 2No unauthorized promotional use: Chapter accounts may not be used to promote individual members’ personal business interests, political campaigns, or any activity not directly approved by the Polemarch and Board of Directors.
  • 3Credit all third-party content: Quotations, images, and intellectual property from third parties must be appropriately credited. Use of copyrighted materials without credit is strictly prohibited.
  • 4No duplicate or plagiarized content: Do not copy content directly from other chapter websites or social media pages without attribution. The chapter’s digital presence should reflect its own unique story and programs.
  • 5No esoteric or confidential content: Ritual content, intake-related information, and confidential fraternity business must never appear on any digital platform in any form. This is a separate prohibition from the Code of Conduct’s electronic communication rule — both apply simultaneously.
  • 6No prohibited imagery: No alcoholic imagery, drug paraphernalia, sexually explicit content, or the Playboy bunny logo in any form may appear on any chapter digital platform.

Chapter Website Standards

If the chapter maintains a website, it must meet the following minimum standards. The chapter Polemarch, Vice Polemarch, and Keeper of Records are jointly responsible for ensuring compliance.

Accessibility & Responsiveness

The website must be usable across different devices (desktop, mobile, tablet), browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge), and by users with disabilities. Web accessibility is not optional — it is a fraternity standard that ensures all community members can access the chapter’s digital presence regardless of ability or device.

Navigation Standards

  • All links (internal and external) must work properly — no broken links
  • Main navigation must be consistent on every page
  • The site must not depend on the browser’s back button for navigation
  • Pages must load quickly; all graphics must be optimized for web delivery
  • No unsolicited pop-ups or auto-play audio or video of any kind
  • The chapter’s name must appear in the title of every page for bookmarking purposes

Required Content

  • The fraternity’s position on hazing must be clearly stated on the website
  • Current contact information for the chapter must be displayed
  • Information about the Grand Chapter, province, and local chapter must be present
  • The website must include information on how to become a member of Kappa Alpha Psi
  • Chapter programs must be described with current, accurate information — outdated content must be removed promptly

Content & Design Standards

  • Proper grammar and spelling throughout — no spelling errors, punctuation mistakes, or factual inaccuracies
  • No content that could be construed as distasteful, offensive, or profane
  • All graphics must be crisp and visually appropriate; no inappropriate graphics
  • Consistent design layout across all pages; user-friendly, simple interface
  • Page length should be reasonable — pages longer than four “page downs” should include “Return to Top” links
  • Only official Kappa Alpha Psi digital images supplied by the fraternity may be used. Second-generation photography or images downloaded from unauthorized sources are prohibited

Trademark & Branding Compliance

Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc. holds registered trademarks on its name, Greek letters (KΦΠ), Coat of Arms, and other insignia. The following restrictions apply to all chapter digital and physical communications.

Absolute prohibition: The Playboy rabbit head logo or any facsimile resembling a rabbit head is strictly prohibited on any website, flyer, correspondence, or other material related to Kappa Alpha Psi. This prohibition is based on a July 2004 order from Past Grand Polemarch Sam Hamilton and carries financial penalties including fines and suspension of the chapter’s charter.

Permitted Uses

  • Official fraternity colors: Crimson (PMS #199C) and Cream (PMS #461C). Red and white are acceptable alternatives for printed materials.
  • The fraternity name and Greek letters when used in direct connection with official chapter business — always with the registered trademark symbol (®)
  • Images and photographs downloaded directly from the official Kappa Media Kit (kappamediakit.com) with proper attribution and subject to the media kit’s terms

Prohibited Uses

  • The fraternity’s badge (Diamond) or any facsimile of the badge on programs, articles of wear, stationery, announcements, or invitations (Statute 35, Section 7)
  • The Coat of Arms reproduced without the registered trademark; the fraternity’s name without the registered trademark symbol
  • Any sexually explicit content, including “Kappa Diamonds” imagery, “Girls of the Month Club,” or similar content
  • Alcoholic imagery, drug paraphernalia, or any content that contradicts the fraternity’s values and standards
  • Second-generation photography (images copied from another source rather than downloaded directly from authorized fraternity media)

Chapter Website Certification

Before a chapter website goes live or after significant updates, the chapter must submit its website link to the Grand Chapter along with contact information for primary and secondary webmasters. A signed certification document — signed by the Polemarch, Vice Polemarch, Keeper of Records, Keeper of Exchequer, and all webmasters — must confirm that:

  • The “bunny rabbit” logo does not appear anywhere on the website, in any flyer, or in any correspondence related to the chapter
  • The website does not contain sexually explicit songs, images, or resemblances to prohibited content
  • The website does not contain alcoholic imagery or drug paraphernalia

If the chapter operates a Kappa League with its own website, the same certification and webmaster contact information must be submitted for that site as well.

Understand the rules governing licensed vendors, permitted color combinations, prohibited items, and the proper use of fraternity images and trademarks on apparel and merchandise.

Overview

All apparel and souvenir retailers and manufacturers must hold an active license from Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc. to produce items bearing the fraternity’s trademarks. Brothers should only purchase from licensed vendors. Any intellectual property violations must be referred immediately to the Vendor Management Team at IHQ.

"The Kappa Alpha Psi® image is more than just the Greek letters KΑΨ and our commitment to Achievement through ΦNΠ. The Kappa Alpha Psi® brand is not only who we are as an organization but also who we want to be as leaders and how we are perceived by the world — all at the same time."

Official Kappa Alpha Psi apparel is sold exclusively through the fraternity’s preferred vendor Nupe Mall (NupeMall.com). Vendor Prohibitions

Any vendor found in violation of the following rules will have their vendor’s license revoked immediately and all vendor fees will be forfeited. Kappa Alpha Psi reserves the right to pursue legal action for any license violation.

  • Reproduce the fraternity’s badge (Diamond) or any facsimile thereof on apparel, programs, stationery, announcements, invitations, or any article of wear (Statute 35, Section 7 of the Constitution)
  • Reproduce the Coat of Arms or use the fraternity’s name without the registered trademark symbol (®)
  • Produce or sell undergarments of any kind (workout gear may be permissible with approval)
  • Reproduce or sell items bearing another company’s intellectual property — including the Playboy Bunny logo or any rabbit-head facsimile (strictly prohibited; carries fines and suspension of charter)
  • Produce or sell paddles of any kind, unless in a specifically approved commemorative format (e.g., key chains)
  • Produce, market, or sell alcohol or wine beverages
  • Sell paraphernalia from any other fraternal or Greek-letter organization without a current vending license from that entity
  • Sell any items reproduced illegally (e.g., bootleg CDs, DVDs) without appropriate written permission
